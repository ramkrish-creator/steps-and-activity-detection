The key analogy — a music equaliser
Imagine you're listening to music on a speaker with an equaliser (EQ). The EQ splits the audio into bands: bass (low freq), midrange, treble (high freq). Each knob tells you how much of that frequency is in the song right now.

The Wavelet Transform does exactly this — but for your accelerometer signal. It splits the motion signal into frequency bands and tells you how much energy is in each band.

Key insight: Each human activity creates a different "frequency fingerprint." Walking puts energy in low bands. Running puts energy in high bands. Standing puts energy in no bands. Wavelet transform reveals this fingerprint.
How the DWT actually decomposes a signal
Step 1
Start with raw signal (0–10 Hz at 20 Hz sampling)
Your accelerometer magnitude: √(x²+y²+z²). Contains all frequencies mixed together.
↓
Step 2 — First split (Low-pass + High-pass filters)
Signal is split into two halves
A1 = Low frequencies (0–5 Hz) — smooth part
D1 = High frequencies (5–10 Hz) — fast detail → SAVED as sub-band D1
↓ (only A1 continues)
Step 3 — Second split
A1 is split again
A2 = 0–2.5 Hz → continues
D2 = 2.5–5 Hz → SAVED as sub-band D2 (jogging lives here!)
↓
Step 4 — Third split
A2 is split again
A3 = 0–1.25 Hz → continues
D3 = 1.25–2.5 Hz → SAVED as sub-band D3 (walking lives here!)
↓
Step 5 — Fourth split (final)
A3 is split one last time
A4 = 0–0.6 Hz → SAVED (slow drift / gravity)
D4 = 0.6–1.25 Hz → SAVED (slow walking)
Why Daubechies 4 (db4) wavelet?
The db4 wavelet shape closely resembles a single footstep impact waveform — a sharp rise followed by a decay. This is called wavelet-signal matching. When the wavelet shape matches the signal shape, the DWT coefficients are large and meaningful. Using a wavelet that doesn't look like a footstep would give weak, noisy coefficients.
What is "energy" in signal processing?
In physics, energy = force × distance. In signal processing, we define energy similarly:

Energy of a band = Σ (coefficient²) / number of samples

You square the coefficients because:
  • Squaring makes negative values positive (motion going up or down both contribute)
  • Squaring amplifies large values — a big footstep impact gives far more energy than a small vibration
  • It matches the physics definition of power (signal power = amplitude²)
Why relative energy, not raw energy?
A person running vigorously will have more raw energy everywhere compared to someone walking slowly — even in bands that shouldn't matter. So we normalise:

Relative Energy = Energy(band) / Total Energy across all bands

This gives you the proportion of total motion in each frequency band. Now a tall person and a short person walking normally show the same relative energy pattern — the fingerprint is person-independent.
